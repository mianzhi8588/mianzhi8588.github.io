## Overview

This project explored passive radiative cooling film technology as a green cooling approach that can reduce reliance on conventional air-conditioning or electrical refrigeration. The work focused on material selection, electrospun film preparation, and experimental validation of cooling performance.

## My Role

**Project Leader**

## My Contributions

- Led a team to investigate theoretical solutions for thin films with passive radiative cooling capability.
- Selected candidate materials based on their cooling effectiveness and feasibility for film fabrication.
- Prepared radiative cooling films using electrospinning equipment.
- Conducted controlled experiments by adjusting preparation parameters and comparing film performance.
- Built and tested an insulated-box experimental setup to evaluate cooling effects under sunlight exposure.
- Compared ordinary films and radiative cooling films to validate thermal-performance differences.
- Collaborated with design students to explore potential product forms for automotive roof applications.
